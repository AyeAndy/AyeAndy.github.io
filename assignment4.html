Press-fit Boxes

Instead of milling, I decided to stay home and work within my limited time to make a box out of my 3d printer. In Rhino, I created a very simple panel with indents and extrusions that would fit together.

The first printed parts did not work or fit well. I had made the indents the same exact size from the portruding parts, so I decided to change the sizes of the indents to make them a little bit bigger. I also made the overall width of the parts bigger as well.

In the second try, the parts were fit too loosely so they were falling apart. Finally, I made the decision to adjust back the parts so that indents would be smaller

Finally, I was able to make a box that worked pretty well on holding itself together, as well as keep some materials inside.
